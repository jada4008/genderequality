Bechdel Test

The bechdel test is a test that requires a movie, book, film, etc to

  1. It has to have at least two [named] women in it
  2. Who talk to each other
  3. About something besides a man

The Bechdel Test helps to highlight how little speaking roles and time is given over to women. This is a problem for actresses getting roles and which stories are told through popular works of fiction.

History

The Bechdel Test was created by Allison Bechdel who was a cartoonist for Dykes to Watch Out For. Bechdel also credited Virginia Woolf's esssay A Room Of One's Own published in 1929. The test gained more popularity in the early 2000s.

Popular Movies That Don't Pass

  • The Original Star Wars

    • The two named female charactes, Princess Leia and Aunt Beru, never meet.

  • The Lord of the Rings

    • The female characters never meet because they are in different parts of Middle Earth.

Popular Movies That Do Pass

  • Ferris Bueller's Day Off

    • Jeanie has multiple conversations with other women about things beside men.

  • How to Lose a Guy in 10 Days

    • Women at work discuss various work related and sports topics.

  • Hidden Figures

    • The movie is about three NASA scientists who talk about many different topics.
