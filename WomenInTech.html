Women In Tech

History of Women In Tech

  • Presently, women are employed in STEM jobs half as often as men are.

  • 20% of women who graduate with a STEM degree are unemployed, where only 10% of men are.

  • Women in the workforce earn an average of 77% of their male counterparts' salaries, which also applies to the tech industry.

  • Women hold only 25% of jobs in the technology industry.

Today's Powerful Women In Tech

Sheryl Sandberg
Sheryl Sandberg

Sheryl Sandberg has been the COO of Facebook since 2008. Previously, she had been the Vice President of Global Online Sales and Operations of Google. She currently is on the board at Women for Women International as well as V-Day, the Center for Global Development, and the Walt Disney Company.

Susan Wojcicki
Susan Wojcicki

Susan Wojcicki has been the CEO of Youtube since 2014. She had previously worked with Google, where she rose from the company's first marketing manager to eventually the Senior Vice President of Advertising & Commerce, overseeing projects such as Google Doodles and Google Images as well as the eventual purchase of Youtube. Now, she advocates for several social issues, such as getting more women interested in tech, fighting gender discrimination in the workplace, and expanding paid family leave.

Marissa Mayer
Marissa Mayer

Marissa Mayer, former CEO of Yahoo and Vice President of Google Search Products. After helping Google aquire survey website Zagat, Mayer was appointed as President and CEO of Yahoo. She also was a member of their Board of Directors. During her time at Yahoo, Mayer led the company to acquire Tumblr, and the company's stock price doubled in her first 14 months working there. Also, she advocated for women's rights at Yahoo, changing the maternity leave policy to benefit new parents by extending the leave time.
